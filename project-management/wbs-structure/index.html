Structure & Work Breakdown

Work Breakdown Structure • Scope Decomposition • Work Packages • Parallel Workstreams

Overview

This section of my project management portfolio highlights my ability to bring structure, clarity, and systems‑level reasoning to complex initiatives. I use Work Breakdown Structures (WBS) to transform broad project goals into organized, actionable components that support scheduling, resource planning, and cross‑team coordination. These artifacts demonstrate how I design frameworks that maintain alignment, reduce ambiguity, and enable teams to work in parallel with confidence and clarity.

Work Breakdown Structure Short Paper

This artifact presents the three‑level Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) for the fictional Sanford B. Ladd School redevelopment project. It reflects my ability to decompose a multi‑phase initiative into well‑defined work packages, each aligned with regulatory requirements, funding constraints, and operational realities. The WBS establishes a clear foundation for scheduling, resource allocation, and performance measurement, ensuring that every component of the project is structured, traceable, and strategically organized.

WBS Dictionary

The WBS Dictionary expands each work package into detailed descriptions, responsibilities, dependencies, and compliance considerations. It reinforces clarity and accountability across all project components and ensures that teams understand how their work fits into the broader initiative. The dictionary appears after the references section of the paper and serves as a leadership tool for maintaining consistency, supporting communication, and guiding execution across parallel workstreams.
