Professional Self-Assessment

I am a developer and emerging technical project manager who thrives at the intersection of software engineering, systems thinking, and collaborative leadership. Completing the Computer Science program and building my ePortfolio has given me the opportunity to demonstrate not only my technical abilities, but also the values and professional mindset that guide my work. Throughout this program, I have strengthened my ability to design secure, maintainable systems, communicate clearly with diverse stakeholders, and lead teams with empathy, structure, and strategic intent. These experiences have shaped my professional goals and prepared me to contribute meaningfully in technical and project-driven environments.

My coursework has allowed me to showcase a wide range of strengths across the software development lifecycle. In programming-intensive courses such as Programming Languages, DSA: Analysis and Design, Computational Graphics and Visualization, and Mobile Architecture & Programming, I developed skills in C++, Python, Java, OpenGL, and Android development. In DSA: Analysis and Design, for example, I implemented and compared multiple data structures in both pseudocode and C++, evaluating their performance using Big O analysis. These experiences helped me learn to write clean, modular, and well-documented code while deepening my understanding of how algorithmic choices affect performance, how data structures influence efficiency, and how mathematical reasoning supports everything from mesh generation to physics simulation. This foundation has made me a more thoughtful and deliberate engineer who can evaluate trade-offs and design solutions that balance performance, scalability, and clarity.

My work in software engineering and database courses, including Operating Platforms, System Analysis and Design, Client/Server Development, and Full Stack Development with MEAN, strengthened my ability to design full systems rather than isolated components. For example, in Full Stack Development with MEAN, I built an end-to-end travel management application using Angular, Express, and MongoDB, integrating reusable UI components and secure authentication. In Client/Server Development, I created a reusable CRUD module in Python that connected dashboard widgets to a MongoDB database, demonstrating modular design and schema-aware validation. These courses also allowed me to gain valuable experience with UML diagrams, requirements analysis, and architectural decision-making. Together, these skills have prepared me to approach software design with a holistic mindset, ensuring that each layer of a system aligns with user needs, technical constraints, and long-term maintainability.

Security has been a consistent theme throughout my program. In Software Security and Software Test, Automation & QA, I learned how to identify vulnerabilities, apply secure coding practices, and use testing strategies to validate functionality and resilience. In Software Security, for example, I conducted a full vulnerability assessment of a financial services application, refactored insecure code, and implemented stronger encryption and dependency checks. These experiences reinforced my understanding of encryption, static analysis, and defensive programming, and they shaped my belief that security is not an afterthought but a core responsibility of every developer. I now approach every project with a security-first mindset that anticipates risks, protects user data, and reinforces trust.

Collaboration and communication have been equally important to my development. Courses like Software Development Lifecycle, System Analysis and Design, Managing/Leading in Business, and Project Management helped me refine my ability to work effectively in team environments, communicate with stakeholders, and lead projects with clarity and purpose. In Software Development Lifecycle, I practiced translating high-level user needs into detailed user stories and acceptance criteria, gained hands-on experience with Agile sprint planning by breaking requirements into iterations and estimating effort, and strengthened my documentation skills through writing requirement specifications, test plans, and process documentation that supported clear communication across technical and non-technical stakeholders. In Project Management, I developed a comprehensive project plan with scope, timeline, risk analysis, and stakeholder communication strategies. In Managing/Leading in Business, I analyzed organizational culture and leadership dynamics, producing managerial recommendations grounded in emotional intelligence and ethical decision-making. These experiences strengthened my leadership style, which is grounded in empathy, transparency, and a commitment to helping teams thrive. They also reinforced my passion for technical project management, where I can combine my engineering background with my strengths in organization, communication, and strategic thinking.

Developing my ePortfolio has been an opportunity to bring all of these skills together. It demonstrates my ability to build mobile applications, design full stack systems, implement custom algorithms, and integrate secure database solutions. It also reflects my growth as a communicator and leader who can explain technical decisions, evaluate trade-offs, and present work in a clear and professional way. The process of curating my artifacts has helped me articulate my strengths, refine my goals, and recognize the value I bring to technical teams.

As I move forward in my career, I am committed to building systems that work, teams that thrive, and solutions that matter. I aim to continue growing as a technical project manager and developer who can bridge the gap between engineering and leadership. My education has prepared me to contribute to complex, interdisciplinary projects with confidence, curiosity, and a strong ethical foundation. I am ready to bring my skills in software engineering, security, data structures, communication, and collaborative leadership into a professional environment where I can support innovation and drive meaningful impact.
